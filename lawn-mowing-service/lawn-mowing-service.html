Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I mow my lawn in Alabama?

During the growing season (April–October), most Alabama lawns need mowing every 7–14 days. Weekly is ideal for fast-growing Bermuda grass. Biweekly works for slower growers or cooler months. In winter, mowing is rarely needed.

What’s included in your mowing service?

Every mowing visit includes full property mowing, edging along driveways and sidewalks, string trimming around fences and obstacles, and blowing off hard surfaces. Your yard looks clean and finished when we leave.

Do you mow wet grass?

We avoid mowing in heavy rain because wet grass doesn’t cut cleanly and can damage the lawn. If rain delays your scheduled visit, we’ll reschedule within 1–2 days and let you know.

How much does lawn mowing cost?
