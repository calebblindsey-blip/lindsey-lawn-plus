Weekly vs. Biweekly — Which Is Right?

Weekly service is ideal during Alabama's peak growing season (April–September) when Bermuda and Zoysia grass grow fast. Weekly mowing keeps the lawn at a consistent height and prevents the "shaggy then scalped" cycle that damages grass.

Biweekly service works well for slower-growing yards, cooler months (October–March), or customers on a tighter budget. Your lawn won't look as consistently tight as weekly, but it's a solid option that still keeps things maintained.

Many of our customers do weekly April through September, then switch to biweekly October through March. We'll help you figure out what works best for your yard and budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pause my recurring service?

Yes. If you’re going out of town or want to pause during winter, just let us know. No penalty, no hassle. Pick back up whenever you’re ready.

What if it rains on my scheduled day?

We reschedule rain days within 1–2 days and let you know. We don’t mow in heavy rain because it damages the lawn and doesn’t cut cleanly.

Do I need to sign a contract?

No. Lindsey Lawn Plus does not require contracts. We earn your business every visit. Cancel or pause anytime with no fees or penalties.

What’s included in each recurring visit?

Every visit includes full property mowing, edging along driveways and sidewalks, string trimming around fences and obstacles, and blowing off hard surfaces.
