Keep Your Shrubs Healthy and Shaped

Overgrown shrubs make even a well-mowed lawn look unkempt. Regular trimming keeps your plants healthy, maintains their shape, and dramatically improves your property's curb appeal. Lindsey Lawn Plus provides professional shrub and bush trimming across North Jefferson County.

We trim seasonally — most shrubs in Alabama benefit from trimming in late spring after the first growth flush, and again in late summer or early fall before dormancy. We'll recommend the right schedule for your specific plants.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should shrubs be trimmed?

Most shrubs in North Alabama benefit from 2–3 trimmings per year — typically in late spring, mid-summer, and early fall. Some fast-growing species like privet may need more frequent attention. We’ll recommend a schedule based on your plants.

Do you trim crepe myrtles?

Yes — properly. We prune crepe myrtles to encourage healthy growth and blooming, not the flat-top "crepe murder" style. Proper trimming keeps them beautiful for years.

Can I add shrub trimming to my mowing service?

Absolutely. Most of our shrub trimming customers are already on a mowing schedule. We handle both so your whole property stays maintained without extra hassle.
