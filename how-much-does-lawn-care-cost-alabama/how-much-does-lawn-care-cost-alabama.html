What Affects the Price?

Yard size is the biggest factor. A 1/4 acre lot costs less than a full acre. Terrain matters too — hills, slopes, and tight spaces take longer. Obstacles like fences, playground equipment, and garden beds add trimming time. And frequency — recurring customers typically pay less per visit than one-time jobs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does lawn mowing cost per visit in Alabama?

Most residential mowing in North Jefferson County runs $35–$75 per visit. Small lots (under 1/4 acre) are typically $35–$50. Medium lots (1/4–1/2 acre) run $50–$75. Larger properties are $75+ depending on size and terrain.

Is biweekly mowing cheaper than weekly?

Per visit, biweekly may be the same price or slightly higher because the grass is taller and takes more work. But your monthly total is lower since you’re getting half as many visits. Many customers find weekly is actually better value because the lawn looks consistently good.

Do you charge extra for the first mow?

If your lawn is significantly overgrown (above 8–10 inches), the first mow may cost more because it takes extra time and passes. After that, regular mowing is at your normal quoted rate. We’ll always tell you upfront.
